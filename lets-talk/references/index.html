Water Usage & Trust

[1]
"42% of Philadelphia residents already use bottled water"
Source: Philadelphia Water Department Surveys (2023)
Details: According to PWD's own surveys, bottled water consumption rebounded to 42% in recent years. More than 600,000 Philadelphians opt to drink bottled water rather than tap water.
Reference: WHYY: "Contamination scare may have made it harder for Philly to build trust in its water" (May 2023)
[2]
"March 2023 Delaware River chemical spill"
Source: Multiple news sources and Wikipedia
Details: On March 24, 2023, the Trinseo plant in Bristol, PA had an equipment failure resulting in a leak of 8,100-12,000 gallons of latex chemicals (butyl acetate, ethyl acetate, and methyl methacrylate) into Otter Creek, a tributary of the Delaware River. The incident led to widespread panic buying of bottled water in Philadelphia. Trinseo ultimately agreed to a $2.7 million settlement.
References:
  • Wikipedia: 2023 Delaware River chemical spill
  • Philadelphia Water Department Response
  • Philadelphia Inquirer Coverage (March 27, 2023)
  • ACEP Toxicology: "A Sticky Situation: The Delaware Latex Spill"

Philadelphia Beverage Tax

[6]
"Philadelphia's 1.5¢/oz beverage tax and its impact"
Verified Data:
  • Tax rate: 1.5 cents per ounce (implemented January 2017)
  • Applies to: Sugar-sweetened AND artificially sweetened beverages
  • Exempt: Pure water, 100% fruit juice, milk products
  • In-store sales decline: 50% for taxed beverages
  • Net decline (after cross-border shopping): 35% in 2018
  • Price increase: Average 1.02 cents/oz (68% pass-through rate)
  • Consumer behavior: 31% reduction in soda consumption frequency
References:
  • City of Philadelphia: Beverage Tax Details
  • NIH Study: Sustained Impact of Philadelphia Beverage Tax (2022)
  • PubMed: Impact on purchases and consumption (2019)
